Tourist Spots and Attractive Destinations in Alappuzha

Alleppey Backwaters

Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is renowned for its enchanting backwaters, interconnected canals, and serene waterways. Houseboat cruises on the backwaters offer a unique and relaxing experience. The lush landscapes and traditional villages along the water route provide a glimpse into the rural life of Kerala.

Marari Beach

Marari Beach is a tranquil and idyllic stretch of coastline known for its pristine white sands and clear blue waters. The beach is perfect for unwinding, taking leisurely strolls, and enjoying the stunning sunsets. Marari offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Kuttanad

Kuttanad is often referred to as the "Rice Bowl of Kerala" due to its vast paddy fields and unique farming practices below sea level. The picturesque landscape is crisscrossed by canals and is best explored by taking a leisurely boat ride. Kuttanad offers a glimpse into the traditional agricultural lifestyle of the region.

Pathiramanal Island

Pathiramanal Island is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. The island's lush greenery and tranquil surroundings provide a habitat for various migratory birds. A boat ride to the island offers a serene escape and the opportunity to observe the rich avian diversity.

Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple

Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple is an ancient temple known for its exquisite architecture and religious significance. The temple is famous for its 'Palpayasam,' a sweet milk porridge offered as prasad. The annual festival, Ambalappuzha Palpayasam, attracts devotees from far and wide.

Alappuzha Beach

Alappuzha Beach is a popular destination for both tourists and locals. The long sandy beach is perfect for leisurely walks, beach sports, and relaxation. The historic Alappuzha Lighthouse adds to the charm of the beach.

Champakulam Church

Champakulam Church, also known as St. Mary's Forane Church, is one of the oldest Christian churches in Kerala. The church's architectural beauty and historical significance make it a must-visit site. The annual feast, Champakulam Moolam Perunnal, is celebrated with great pomp and fervor.

Vembanad Lake

Vembanad Lake is the longest lake in India and offers stunning views and a peaceful environment. The lake is a major backwater attraction, and boat races are held during the Onam festival. The surrounding villages and paddy fields create a picturesque setting.
