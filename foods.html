Kerala Foods

Thiruvananthapuram District

  • Sadya: Kerala Sadhya is a traditional vegetarian feast of Keralites. Its spread can go up to 28 dishes at a time. Traditionally, Sadhya is served on a plantain leaf, with the tapering side of the leaf pointing to the left of the guest. It consists of par boiled red rice, side dishes, savouries, pickles and desserts, all served at different times of the meal. Sadhya is a feast of flavours and enriches your palate in many ways.
  • Aviyal: Aviyal, also called Avial, is a popular and traditional mixed vegetable dish from the Kerala cuisine. It is an essential dish of Sadya, a festive vegetarian feast served on a plantain leaf. The dish is made with mixed vegetables, fresh coconut, curry leaves, coconut oil and curd (yogurt). Aviyal is said to have been invented by Bhima, one of the five Pandava brothers from the Mahabharata epic. It is a healthy mix of various vegetables in a coconut and yogurt sauce with a finishing of coconut oil and curry leaves.
  • Kerala Sadya Parippu Curry: Parippu Curry is a popular recipe in Kerala made using dal and is one of the main dishes in the Sadya. Moong dal is cooked well and then concocted with a coconut-chili paste. Finally, the Parippu curry is tempered with coconut oil-based mustard, curry leaves, and shallots. It is a very delicious dal to mix with hot steaming rice. The main uniqueness of this Kerala style dal is the coconut spice paste, which gives a unique flavor to this dal.
  • Kappa and Meen Curry: Tapioca and Meen Curry, also known as Kappa Meen Curry, is a popular dish from the Kerala cuisine. It is a true Keralite affair between the Cassava Root and the red hot (not too spicy) Fish Curry.The dish is made with cooked tapioca tempered with mustard seed, green chili, curry leaves and shallot, served along with a spicy and sour Kerala style fish curry.
  • Ada Pradhaman: Ada Pradhaman is a traditional dessert from Kerala, made using rice ada, coconut milk, and jaggery. Rice Ada is a type of pasta made from rice flour, which is cooked until thick and dough-like, then dried out into thick, long ribbons.
