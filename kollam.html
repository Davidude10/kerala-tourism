Tourist Spots and Attractive Destinations in Kollam

Kollam Beach

Kollam Beach, also known as Mahatma Gandhi Beach, is a scenic and breathtaking natural wonder located in the port city of Kollam, Kerala. It is the first ‘Beach Wedding Destination’ in Kerala and features a park of international standard, the Mahatma Gandhi Park. The beach is popular worldwide for its unexplored beauty, pristine setting, and peaceful ambiance. It is one of the few beaches in Kerala with a lifeguard outpost, ensuring the safety of visitors. The beach is also home to Chinese fishing nets, Chinese water pots, and sampan-like boats, remnants of its past as a bustling harbor for Chinese ships and trade. With a variety of snack stalls and nearby attractions such as Kanyakumari beach and Padmanabhapuram palace, Kollam Beach is an ideal destination for a relaxing and culturally enriching beachside vacation.

Jatayu Earth's Center

Jatayu Earth’s Center, also known as Jatayu Nature Park or Jatayu Rock, is a park and tourism center at Chadayamangalam in the Kollam district of Kerala, India. It stands at an altitude of 350m (1200ft) above the mean sea level. Jatayu Nature Park holds the distinction of having the world’s largest bird sculpture, which is of Jatayu. The sculpture measures 200 feet (61 m) long, 150 feet (46 m) wide, 70 feet (21 m) in height and occupies 15,000 square feet (1,400 m2) of floor area. It was sculpted by Rajiv Anchal. This rock-theme nature park was the first Public–private partnership tourism initiative in the state of Kerala under the BOT model. The park is about 38 km (24 mi) away from the city of Kollam and 46 km (29 mi) away from the state capital, Thiruvananthapuram.

Ashtamudi Lake

Ashtamudi Lake is a large palm-shaped water body located in the Kollam District of Kerala. It is the second largest estuary ecosystem in the state and is well known for its houseboat and backwater resorts. The lake possesses a unique wetland ecosystem and is home to over forty-three types of mangroves. Boat cruises are operated by the Kollam Boat Club from Kollam to Alappuzha, providing transport access to many other towns and villages along this route. The lush greenery and exotic biodiversity of Ashtamudi Lake are sure to enchant visitors forever.

Thangassery Lighthouse

Thangassery Lighthouse, also known as Tangasseri Lighthouse, is situated at Tangasseri in Kollam city of the Indian state of Kerala. It is one of the two lighthouses in the Kollam Metropolitan Area and is maintained by the Cochin Directorate General of Lighthouses and Lightships. The cylindrical lighthouse tower, painted with white and red oblique bands, has a height of 41 meters (135 ft), making it the second tallest lighthouse on the Kerala coast. Thangassery Lighthouse is one of the most visited lighthouses in Kerala.

Thenmala Eco-Tourism

Thenmala Ecotourism is India’s first planned eco-tourism destination, located in the Kollam district of Kerala. It has 10 eco-tourism spots that cover the great hill ranges of Thiruvananthapuram, Pathanamthitta, and Kollam districts. The name “Thenmala” translates to “Honey Hill” and high-quality honey is exported from this area. The surrounding dense forest is also known for its timber. Thenmala offers a variety of activities, including boat rides, leisurely walks, mountain biking, and rock climbing. The staff is well trained in providing the required help and information at all times.

Alumkadavu Backwaters

Alumkadavu is a small village in the town of Karunagapally, just 23 km from Kollam, Kerala. It is the hub of the Kettuvallam building industry, with hundreds of artisans busy at work putting together traditional houseboats like it has been done for hundreds of years. At Alumkadavu, you might get a chance to see a floating conference hall, perhaps the only one of its kind in the world. The village is also home to Kerala’s first houseboat/kettuvallam and is a renowned backwater tourism destination where one can experience the soul of God’s Own Country. The refreshing fragrance of coir, wood, and bamboos marks this beautiful village. The place is acclaimed for making Kerala’s traditional houseboats eco-friendly as well as being the key manufacturer. Besides these specialties, the place possesses idyllic natural beauty and exotic flora and fauna that will refresh and rejuvenate you.

Munroe Island

Munroe Island, also known as Mundrothuruthu, is an inland island group located at the confluence of Ashtamudi Lake and the Kallada River, in Kollam district, Kerala, South India. It is a group of eight small islets comprising a total area of about 13.4 km². The island is accessible by road, rail, and inland water navigation and is about 25 km from Kollam by road. The place is named in honor of Resident Colonel John Munroe of the former Princely State of Travancore. Munroe Island is a hidden pearl in the backwaters which is composed of a cluster of 8 islands, each separated by small water channels and lakes.
