HOW TO REACH KERALA

Kerala International Airports
Trivandrum International Airport (Thiruvananthapuram Airport)

IATA Code: TRV

Airlines: Major domestic and international airlines such as Air India, IndiGo, Emirates, Qatar Airways, Etihad Airways, and more operate flights to and from Trivandrum International Airport. It serves destinations in the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and other international locations.

Cochin International Airport

IATA Code: COK

Airlines: Cochin International Airport is one of the busiest airports in India. It is served by numerous domestic and international airlines, including Air India, Emirates, Singapore Airlines, Qatar Airways, and many others. It offers a wide range of international and domestic connections.

Calicut International Airport (Karipur Airport)

IATA Code: CCJ

Airlines: Calicut International Airport connects Kerala to several Middle Eastern destinations and domestic airports. Airlines like Air India Express, Oman Air, and others operate flights to and from this airport.

Kannur International Airport

IATA Code: CNN

Airlines: Kannur International Airport is a relatively new addition to Kerala's airports. It offers flights to domestic destinations within India and connects to international locations in the Middle East and Southeast Asia. Airlines like Air India Express, IndiGo, and others operate here.

Kerala Cruise Ports
Kochi (Cochin) Port

Location: Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a major port city in Kerala, located on the southwest coast of India.

Cruise Terminal: Kochi has a dedicated cruise terminal known as the Ernakulam Wharf. This modern facility is equipped to handle cruise ships and provides services to cruise passengers.

Cruise Lines: Several international cruise lines, including Royal Caribbean, Costa Cruises, MSC Cruises, and others, have included Kochi as a port of call in their itineraries. Some luxury cruise lines also offer cruises that explore the Indian subcontinent and make stops in Kochi.

Thiruvananthapuram (Trivandrum) Port

Location: Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala, situated on the southern tip of the state.

Cruise Terminal: Thiruvananthapuram has a cruise terminal that can accommodate smaller and mid-sized cruise ships.

Cruise Lines: While Thiruvananthapuram is not as frequently visited by cruise lines as Kochi, some smaller luxury cruise lines and expedition cruise companies include it in their itineraries, offering passengers the opportunity to explore the city and nearby attractions.

Traveling to Kerala by Road
Traveling from Sri Lanka

Ferry Services: You can reach Kerala from Sri Lanka by taking a ferry service. There are passenger ferries that operate between Colombo (Sri Lanka) and Kochi (Kerala), making it a unique and scenic way to enter the state. The ferry journey offers beautiful views of the Indian Ocean and the coast.

Traveling from Other Indian States

Karnataka and Tamil Nadu: Kerala shares its land borders with the Indian states of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Travelers can enter Kerala by road through well-maintained highways and road networks connecting these states. Popular entry points include Walayar, Palakkad District (from Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu) and Mananthavady, Wayanad District (from Mysore, Karnataka).

Checkpoints: When crossing state borders within India, travelers may encounter border checkpoints. Make sure to carry proper identification and travel documents such as driver's license, vehicle registration, and proof of identity.

CELEBRATE VIBRANT FESTIVALS

Experience the rich culture of Kerala through its festivals and events:

Thrissur Pooram
Thrissur Pooram is one of the most spectacular temple festivals in Kerala. It features a grand procession of decorated elephants, traditional music, and a mesmerizing display of fireworks. The event takes place in Thrissur and attracts thousands of spectators.
Nenmara Vallanghy Vela
Nenmara Vallanghy Vela is a unique festival held in Nenmara and Vallanghy villages of Palakkad. The highlight is a colorful procession of caparisoned elephants accompanied by traditional percussion music and cultural performances.
Arattupuzha Pooram
Arattupuzha Pooram is another famous temple festival held in Arattupuzha, Thrissur district. It's known for its grand elephant procession and traditional art forms like Kudamattam (changing of colorful parasols) and Panchavadyam (orchestral performance).
Kerala Boat Races
Kerala is renowned for its boat races, with the Nehru Trophy Boat Race in Alleppey being the most famous. These races feature long, colorful snake boats and are a thrilling spectacle.
Makaravilakku
This festival at the Sabarimala Temple is known for the appearance of the Makara Jyothi, a divine light on the horizon. Pilgrims gather to witness this celestial event and offer prayers.
Theyyam
Exclusive to northern Kerala, Theyyam is a ritualistic dance form where performers transform into deities through elaborate costumes and makeup, combining spirituality and artistry.
Kalpathi Ratholsavam
The chariot festival in Kalpathi, Palakkad, is a grand celebration featuring beautifully decorated temple chariots, traditional music, and dance performances. It's one of the oldest festivals in Kerala.
Thrikarthika
Celebrated with the lighting of lamps and candles, especially in Kozhikode (Calicut), Thrikarthika creates a beautiful sight as homes and temples are illuminated.
Padayani
This traditional folk art from central Kerala is characterized by vibrant masks and costumes. It's performed during temple festivals and depicts various mythological stories.

SAVOUR KERALA'S DELICIOUS FOODS

Indulge in authentic Kerala dishes:

Pazhampori

Pazhampori, also known as banana fritters, are deep-fried ripe banana slices coated in a spiced batter.

Uzhunnuvada

Uzhunnuvada, or urad dal fritters, are crispy and savory snacks made from ground black gram lentils.

Parippuvada

Parippuvada is a popular tea-time snack in Kerala, made from spiced lentil batter and deep-fried to perfection.

Sughiyan

Sughiyen is a delightful Kerala snack, a sweet and savory mix of ripe banana and jaggery.

Samosa

Although originally from North India, samosas are a common sight in Kerala's tea shops. These triangular pastries are filled with spiced potatoes and peas.

Cutlet

Kerala cutlets are a delicious snack made from minced meat or vegetables, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried to perfection.

Puffs

Kerala bakery puffs are savory pastries filled with a variety of fillings like spiced meat , egg or vegetables. They're a popular snack choice.

Achappam

Achappam is a traditional Kerala snack, known for its intricate flower-like shape. It's made from rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar.

KNOW THE CULTURE

Discover the unique traditions and customs of Kerala:

Kalaripayattu
Kalaripayattu is one of the oldest martial arts forms in the world, originating in Kerala. It includes a combination of physical techniques, weaponry, and healing methods. This ancient art form has had a significant influence on other martial arts around the world.
Onam
Onam is the state festival of Kerala celebrated with great enthusiasm. It involves traditional dance, music, and a grand feast called "Onasadya." This vibrant festival is a time for communities to come together and showcase the rich cultural heritage of Kerala.
Kathakali
Kathakali is a highly stylized and elaborate form of classical Indian dance-drama known for its colorful makeup, intricate costumes, and expressive movements. It often depicts mythological stories through its performances.
Mohiniyattam
Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form of Kerala performed by women. It is characterized by graceful movements and intricate hand gestures, and it often portrays stories of love and devotion.
Ayurveda
Kerala is renowned for its Ayurvedic traditions, offering holistic healing and wellness treatments. Ayurveda spas and resorts are popular among tourists seeking rejuvenation and relaxation.
Theyyam
Theyyam is a vibrant ritualistic dance form in northern Kerala, where performers transform into deities through elaborate costumes and makeup. It's a unique blend of spirituality and artistry.
Traditional Music
Kerala has a rich tradition of music, including classical forms like Carnatic music, as well as folk music and instruments like the "Chenda" and "Mridangam." Music is an integral part of Kerala's culture.
Attukal Pongala
Attukal Pongala is an annual festival where millions of women gather to prepare a special offering to the goddess Attukal Bhagavathy, creating a Guinness World Record for the largest congregation of women. It's a symbol of devotion and unity.
